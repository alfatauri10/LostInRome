LostInRome

Our itinerary was born as part of a school project aimed at discovering and showcasing Rome's historical heritage in our own way. We've selected six of the Eternal City's most significant monuments, each representing a different era and aspect of its millennia-old history.

by Jacopo
General Information
Total duration: 1 full day
Total distance: ~6 km (on foot)
Difficulty: Easy (urban route)
Base budget: €30-50 (entrances and snacks)
Comfort budget: €60-80 (entrances, sit-down meals, aperitifs)
Recommended Schedule
08:30-10:00 Altare della Patria
10:00-11:00 Trevi Fountain + breakfast
11:30-13:00 Pantheon + lunch
13:30-14:30 Ara Pacis
15:00-17:00 Spanish Steps + aperitif
17:30-19:00 Piazza del Popolo + sunset
1
Altare della Patria (Piazza Venezia)

The monument symbolizing united Italy, with a breathtaking panoramic terrace.

▼

Information

Location: Piazza Venezia
Panoramic terrace 5/18€ (elevator included)
Hours: 9:30-19:30 (last entry 18:45)
Tip: Arrive early to avoid lines and enjoy the view in the morning with perfect light for photos!
Experience

We recommend starting your visit by going up to the stunning panoramic terrace of the Altare della Patria: from here you can enjoy a breathtaking view over the rooftops of the city. A truly unmissable spectacle!

Next: Via del Corso (10 min)
2
Trevi Fountain and breakfast

Rome's most famous fountain and a delicious Roman breakfast.

▼

Information

Breakfast: Antico Forno Di Trevi
Average cost: €4-8 for breakfast
Underground tour: €15-20 (recommended)
Tip: If you still have some energy at the end of the day, we recommend visiting the Trevi Fountain late at night: you'll find it completely deserted, illuminated by soft lights creating a magical atmosphere, truly unmissable.
Experience

Continuing along Via del Corso, you'll reach the famous Trevi Fountain, perfect for taking a nice photo and, of course, for making a wish. If you have time, we highly recommend taking a fascinating underground tour that will take you to discover the ancient aqueduct that still feeds the fountain today.

Breakfast: At Antico Forno Di Trevi for a typical Roman breakfast with cornetti and cappuccino.
Next: Via delle Muratte and Via del Seminario (8 min)
3
Pantheon

The temple of the gods and a leap into Roman eternity.

▼

Information

Snack: Roman deli
Average cost: €5
To visit the monument: RESERVATION REQUIRED
buy your ticket here: €2-5
Tip: A few minutes' walk away, don't miss the Church of San Luigi dei Francesi, which houses three magnificent paintings by Caravaggio
Experience

Continuing along Via del Corso, you'll reach the famous Trevi Fountain, perfect for taking a nice photo and, of course, for making a wish. If you have time, we highly recommend taking a fascinating underground tour that will take you to discover the ancient aqueduct that still feeds the fountain today.

Quick lunch: Roman deli: focaccia with porchetta, truffle and other delights — only 10€.
Next: Via di Ripetta (9 min)
4
Ara Pacis

The marble that tells of peace more powerful than war

▼

Information

To visit the monument: RESERVATION REQUIRED
Buy your ticket here: Price subject to change.
Tip: The afternoon light enhances the marble reliefs, ideal for photographs.
Experience

By booking your ticket online, you can access the museum where expert guides will take you to discover this important monument. The Ara Pacis, however, is also clearly visible from the outside thanks to the large windows that surround it.

Don't miss: The panels with Aeneas and the Capitoline Wolf, and the extraordinary floral decoration that envelops the entire altar.
Next: 15 min walk
5
Spanish Steps

For centuries Rome's drawing room, where history, art and nobility meet at the foot of the staircase 🏛️🌸.

▼

Information

Aperitif: Terrazza del palazzetto overlooking the Trinità dei Monti staircase
Average cost: €15-30
For menu and prices: Click here
Lunch: Pastificio Guerra for takeaway pasta dishes
Average cost: €5-6
Tip: Climb to the top for a splendid photo with Rome at your feet 📸
Experience

Here you can refresh yourself by filling your water bottle with drinking water from the Barcaccia Fountain. Once refreshed, climb the scenic Trinità dei Monti staircase to admire the square from a truly suggestive perspective.

Breakfast: At Antico Forno Di Trevi for a typical Roman breakfast with cornetti and cappuccino.
Next: Via del Babuino though it's worth making a detour to Via Margutta, the street of artists (10 min)
6
Piazza del Popolo

Where Rome's history reveals itself in every corner, an ancient gateway that welcomed travelers for centuries 🏛️⚔️

▼

Information

Aperitif: Terrazza del Palazzetto overlooking the Trinità dei Monti staircase
Average cost: €15-30
For menu and prices: Click here
Lunch: Pastificio Guerra for takeaway pasta dishes
Average cost: €5-6
Tip: While at Piazza del Popolo, try speaking from one side of the square to the other: its elliptical shape and the arrangement of the monuments create exceptional acoustics that will allow you to clearly hear even a whisper from afar. A fun and surprising experience!
Experience

From Piazza del Popolo take Viale Gabriele D'Annunzio: on the curve you'll find a staircase that will lead you to one of the most fascinating panoramic views of Rome, that of the Pincio. The romantic atmosphere and breathtaking view will captivate you. You are now in the heart of Villa Borghese. Here you can relax on the grass or, for a different experience, rent a small boat on the lake for just €3 per person.

Breakfast: At Antico Forno Di Trevi for a typical Roman breakfast with cornetti and cappuccino.

Adventure

From the cobblestones of the Appia Antica, where history grazes your bike wheels, to the secret vertigo of the Buco della serratura and the scents of the Orange Garden. Then fly among the cherry blossoms of the Japanese Garden, a haiku of petals and peace, before diving into the tribal rhythms of the Voodoo Bar, where cocktails burn like sunsets over the Colosseum and the DJ makes emperors' ghosts tremble.

Pedal. Explore. Sip. Dance.

by Jacopo
General Information
Total duration: 1 full day
Total distance: ~25 km (by bike and on foot)
Difficulty: Medium (some steep climbs)
Base budget: €50-70 (standard bike, quick lunch, only aperitif)
Comfort budget: €80-100 (e-bike, sit-down meal, premium cocktail)
Recommended Schedule

1
Appia Antica by bike

From catacombs to Terme di Caracalla, between history, nature and archaeology. Where every stone tells a story 🚲

▼

Information

Start: Porta San Sebastiano
End: Terme di Caracalla
Length: ~12 km
Duration: 3 hours (with stops)
Difficulty: Easy (mixed terrain)
Transport: Bike
Bike rental:
Appia Antica Bike Rental (€15/day MTB, €25 e-bike)
Ecobike Roma (€10/day)
Tip: Start at 9:00 to avoid the summer sun and enjoy brunch in peace! 🌿☕
Itinerary Stops
1
Porta di San Sebastiano and Catacombs of San Callisto
30 min 8 1.5 km Show on map

Start from the majestic Porta San Sebastiano (entrance to the Appia Antica) and bike 1.5 km (5 min by bike) to the Catacombs of San Callisto, among the largest in Rome.

Audioguide included

Hours: 9:00-12:00 / 14:00-17:00

Attention: No photography allowed

2
Brunch at Giardino di Lù
€12-20 9:00-16:00 1h30 Show on map

Organic brunch with savory pies, smoothies and artisanal coffee in a hidden garden with a view of the Appia antica.

Must try: Zucchini and ricotta savory pie + mint smoothie

Reservation: +39 06 1234567 (recommended on weekends)

Pets: Dogs allowed in garden

Address: Via Appia Antica, 42

3
Mausoleum of Cecilia Metella
20 min 6 3 km Show on map

Continue along the paved road to the symbol of the Appia Antica: the Mausoleum of Cecilia Metella (1st century BC tomb) and the attached Castrum Caetani (medieval fortress).

Combined ticket: €6 with Villa dei Quintili (valid 24h)

Photography: Allowed without flash

Panoramic view from the fortress terrace
4
Villa dei Quintili
30 min 5 4 km Show on map

1 km detour from the via Appia Antica to visit this luxurious imperial residence with mosaics and private baths.

Picnic area: Available in the garden

Tip: Little shade, bring a hat

Surface: 24 hectares (visit only main areas)

5
Terme di Caracalla
40 min 8 5 km Show on map

End the tour with one of the most grandiose ancient bath complexes, a masterpiece of Roman engineering.

Evening visits: July/August with special lighting

Events: Opera in summer (separate program)

Accessibility: Disabled paths available
Transfer : 15 min by bike (or bus 118) to Aventino
